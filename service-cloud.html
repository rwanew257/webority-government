Frequently Asked Questions

MeghRaj GI Cloud (Government of India Cloud) is the Government of India’s sovereign cloud infrastructure managed by NIC. It is the mandated or preferred hosting environment for Central Ministry and State Government digital applications, ensuring 100% data sovereignty within India, compliance with DPDP Act 2023 data localisation requirements, and alignment with NIC information security policy. Webority provides specialist MeghRaj GI Cloud services including architecture design, deployment, and 24×7 managed services.

NIC National Data Centres (NDCs) are the Government of India’s sovereign Tier IV data centre facilities located in Delhi, Hyderabad, Pune, and Bhubaneswar. They provide the physical hosting for MeghRaj GI Cloud and direct NIC-hosted applications. Webority architects, deploys, and manages government applications on NIC NDC infrastructure — ensuring compliance with NIC information security policies, GoI data residency requirements, and DPDP Act 2023 obligations. All applications remain within Indian sovereign infrastructure with no dependency on foreign servers.

Webority’s Annual Maintenance Contract (AMC) for government applications on MeghRaj GI Cloud or NIC infrastructure includes: 24×7 application monitoring and incident management, L1/L2/L3 tiered helpdesk with defined SLA response times, security patch management and version updates, Disaster Recovery and Business Continuity Planning (DR/BCP) testing, performance optimisation, and compliance documentation updates for annual GoI audits. All AMC engagements follow ITIL-aligned service management and align with government budget and payment cycles.
