Project Overview

Technology for India’s Sickle Cell Eradication Mission

Sickle cell anaemia disproportionately affects India’s tribal populations. The Government of India launched the National Sickle Cell Anaemia Eradication Mission with a goal of screening all tribal citizens by 2047. Sickle Scan is the digital backbone of this mission — enabling frontline ASHA workers to screen citizens, record results, and track treatment in some of India’s most remote tribal districts.

The most critical design constraint: the platform had to work in districts with no reliable internet connectivity — including areas where smartphones have never had a stable data connection. Every feature was designed offline-first.

What We Delivered

The platform was built around six integrated components — an offline-first mobile application for ASHA workers, a multilingual interface designed for low-literacy users, screening and pedigree tracking modules, and a central programme dashboard for MoTA officers monitoring screening coverage across India’s tribal districts.

  1. 01.

    Offline-First Mobile Application

    Android application engineered to operate entirely without internet connectivity — local SQLite storage, background sync when connection available, conflict resolution for concurrent field use.

  2. 02.

    Multilingual Interface — 8+ Languages

    Full UI localisation in 8+ Indian languages including Hindi, Odia, Marathi, Chhattisgarhi, and tribal languages — with simplified iconographic UX designed for low-literacy ASHA workers.

  3. 03.

    Citizen Screening & Registration Module

    Digital screening workflow — citizen registration, sickle cell test recording, result classification, and automatic referral triggers for positive cases.

  4. 04.

    Family Pedigree Tracking

    Genealogical tracking module linking family members’ screening records — enabling identification of carrier populations and at-risk family groups for prioritised follow-up.

  5. 05.

    Central Programme Dashboard

    Web-based dashboard for MoTA programme officers — real-time screening coverage by district, positive case counts, referral completion rates, and ASHA worker performance.

  6. 06.

    Treatment & Follow-Up Tracking

    Post-diagnosis tracking of patient referrals, treatment initiation, and follow-up appointments — ensuring no positive case is lost to follow-up.
