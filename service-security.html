Frequently Asked Questions

VAPT (Vulnerability Assessment and Penetration Testing) for government is a structured cybersecurity assessment that identifies and validates security vulnerabilities in government digital infrastructure — web portals, applications, networks, and databases. Government VAPT must be aligned with CERT-In security guidelines and NIC information security policy. Reports are delivered with a formal remediation roadmap suitable for government audit documentation. Webority has conducted VAPT for organisations including Sashastra Seema Bal under the Ministry of Home Affairs — among the most sensitive security environments in India.

Webority Technologies holds ISO 27001:2017 certification for its Information Security Management System (ISMS). This certification governs how the organisation handles all government client data throughout the project lifecycle — covering data classification, access control, incident management, and supplier security. The same ISO 27001:2017 standard is applied when helping government clients achieve their own ISMS certification.

All cybersecurity engagements are designed around the DPDP Act 2023 framework — ensuring data minimisation during security testing, secure handling of vulnerability data, and breach notification protocol documentation as a standard deliverable. Our VAPT methodology includes a DPDP Act 2023 compliance gap analysis as a component of every government cybersecurity assessment. All testing is conducted on isolated environments with NDA and government-grade confidentiality protocols in place.
