Upgrading Without Switching Off

A significant portion of India’s government IT runs on technology that is 10–20 years old — legacy codebases built for desktop environments, siloed databases that cannot integrate with India Stack, and applications that cannot scale to citizen-demand volumes. The risk of doing nothing is system failure; the risk of a poorly managed migration is citizen service disruption during transition.

Government legacy modernisation is the most risk-intensive service in the public sector IT stack. We built our delivery model around one rule: the live government service must remain operational every single day of the migration. Our Analyse → Isolate → Migrate → Validate methodology was designed specifically for this constraint — not adapted from a commercial migration playbook.

Frequently Asked Questions

Legacy system modernisation for government is the structured process of upgrading outdated government IT — old codebases, siloed databases, desktop applications — to modern, interoperable platforms without disrupting live citizen services. It must comply with NIC technical architecture standards, GIGW 3.0, DPDP Act 2023, and enable India Stack integration. Webority delivers this through a four-phase Analyse → Isolate → Migrate → Validate methodology with CMMI Level 5 process governance and STQC-aligned testing.

Webority uses parallel-run architecture during all government legacy migrations — the existing system remains live and continues to serve citizens while the modernised version is built, tested, and validated alongside it. Data is synchronised in real-time during the transition window. Cutover is only authorised after full UAT, STQC-aligned testing, VAPT security assessment, and data integrity validation are complete. This approach has been refined through 13+ years of government IT delivery since 2012.

All legacy modernisation projects delivered by Webority are architected for compatibility with NIC National Data Centre infrastructure and MeghRaj GI Cloud — the Government of India’s sovereign cloud platform. This ensures modernised systems meet GoI data sovereignty requirements, align with DPDP Act 2023 data localisation obligations, and integrate with national digital infrastructure including eOffice, DigiLocker, Aadhaar, and UPI.
