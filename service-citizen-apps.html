Frequently Asked Questions

Offline-first architecture is a core specialisation for Webority Technologies. Our field officer applications use local-first data storage with sync-when-connected architecture — the app remains fully functional in areas with zero internet connectivity and automatically synchronises data when connectivity is restored. This is proven in our Sickle Scan deployment for ASHA workers in remote tribal districts under the Ministry of Tribal Affairs’ National Sickle Cell Mission, and the BEE Field Officer App for market surveillance across State Designated Agencies.

Citizen app development for government requires support for 12+ Indian languages including low-literacy UX patterns, offline-first architecture for rural and tribal deployments, integration with India Stack (Aadhaar, DigiLocker, UPI), and DPDP Act 2023-compliant data handling. These requirements require government-native engineering from the first line of code. Webority has proven this in the most demanding conditions: Sickle Scan for the Ministry of Tribal Affairs, deployed in zero-connectivity tribal districts.

GIGW 3.0 (Guidelines for Indian Government Websites) compliance and WCAG 2.1 Level AA accessibility are baseline requirements — built into our development process from the first design sprint, not verified at the end. This includes screen reader compatibility, keyboard navigation, sufficient colour contrast, multilingual content structure, and MeitY mobile application guidelines compliance. Departments receive a GIGW 3.0 compliance report and WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility audit as standard project deliverables.
